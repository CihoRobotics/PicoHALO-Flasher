How to enter BOOTSEL mode click to expand
↓
Before you start
1
Use Google Chrome (v89 or newer). This flasher uses the Web Serial API which only works in Chrome — not Firefox, Safari, or Edge.
2
Unplug your Pico from USB if it is currently plugged in.
Entering BOOTSEL mode
3
Hold the BOOTSEL button — the small white button on top of the Pico board.
4
While still holding BOOTSEL, plug the USB cable into your computer.
5
Release BOOTSEL. Your Pico now appears as a USB drive: RPI-RP2 (Pico/W) or RP2350 (Pico 2/2W) in your file explorer.
Flashing
6
Select your board and temperature unit above, then click Connect & Flash.
7
A Chrome popup asks you to choose a serial port. Pick the one labelled RP2 Boot or similar and click Connect.
8
The flasher uploads automatically. Do not unplug during this step. Watch the progress bar.
9
When done, the Pico reboots itself and PicoHALO starts automatically. You are finished!
Troubleshooting
!
If it fails, unplug and repeat from step 3. If the Pico does not appear in the port list, try a different USB cable — some cables carry power only and have no data wires.
