Now install it on your Pico
Follow these 3 simple steps — takes about 30 seconds
1
Unplug your Pico from USB if it is currently connected.
2
Hold the BOOTSEL button — the small white button on top of the board — then plug in the USB cable while still holding it. Release the button once plugged in. Your Pico will appear as a USB drive called RPI-RP2 on your computer, just like a USB stick.
3
Open that RPI-RP2 drive and drag the downloaded .uf2 file straight into it. The drive disappears automatically — that means it worked. PicoHALO boots up on its own. You are done!
What you should see
1→Hold BOOTSEL + plug USB
2→RPI-RP2 drive appears on your computer
3→Drag .uf2 into the drive
4→Drive disappears — Pico reboots
5→PicoHALO starts ✓
💡
Tip: If the RPI-RP2 drive does not appear, try a different USB cable. Some cables carry power only and have no data wires — your Pico will charge but won't show up as a drive.
